THE EPA HAS CONCLUDED THAT POLLUTION IS NO LONGER A PROBLEM, WHICH IS CERTAINLY ONE WAY TO ADDRESS POLLUTION

Look, we need to talk about the Environmental Protection Agency—or as they've apparently rebranded, EPA: Everything is Perfectly Awesome. It turns out that having the word "Protection" in your name is a bit like a "Leak-Proof" travel mug that ruins your laptop, or a "Non-Stick" pan that currently holds a permanent collection of your scrambled eggs. It's an optimistic label that has officially lost its connection to the product inside.

Well, on February 12, they finalized the repeal of the Endangerment Finding. For those of you who haven't spent the last 16 years huffing regulatory filings for fun, the Endangerment Finding was the 2009 scientific determination that greenhouse gas emissions — stay with me here — actually endanger public health and welfare. It was the legal load-bearing wall for every major federal climate regulation we have. And the EPA just took a metaphorical chainsaw to it.

How did they justify it? Did they find a secret new atmosphere? Did they discover that CO₂ is actually made of kittens and Vitamin C? No.

  • New Science Conducted: Absolute zero.
  • New Data Collected: None.
  • Atmospheric Consultation: The atmosphere was not invited to the meeting.

Instead, Administrator Lee Zeldin called this "the single largest deregulatory action in U.S. history." Which is a very fancy, D.C. way of saying they decided to solve the climate crisis by simply reframing it. It's not a catastrophe; it's just... "journaling" for federal policy.

"
The Trump EPA's slapdash legal arguments should be laughed out of court... the administration's legal and scientific reasons for doing so are a joke.
Meredith Hankins, Legal Director for Federal Climate, NRDC
NRDC Press Release, Feb 18, 2026

The car rules also scrapped alongside this repeal would have cut nearly 8 billion tons of greenhouse gas emissions over the next three decades — more than the entire US economy emits in a full year. The EPA's own analysis found eliminating these standards will increase gas prices and cost consumers more at the pump. They literally printed the "this will hurt you" part and the "we're doing it anyway" part in the same document.

You also need to know that a federal judge already ruled the administration violated federal law when it secretly assembled a "Climate Working Group" to ghostwrite the scientific basis for this very repeal. The court compelled them to hand over more than 100,000 documents. The ghostwritten science was used to justify overturning the real science. This is a Scooby-Doo villain arc and we mean that with complete sincerity.

The Clean Air Act that underpins all of this was signed by Richard Nixon in 1970. When Nixon is the environmental voice of reason in the room, you know the timeline has officially splintered.

You can repeal a finding, but you can't repeal the physical properties of carbon. The atmosphere continues to not read federal documents, and frankly, I don't blame it.

The Dispatch
Someone, Somewhere, Is Actually Fixing This
Solutions, forward motion, and what to actually watch right now

The Endangerment Finding Is Gone Federally. Here's Where the Fight Actually Lives Now.

On February 12, 2026, the EPA finalized the repeal of its 2009 Endangerment Finding and simultaneously eliminated all carbon emissions standards for light-, medium-, and heavy-duty vehicles. It is a significant blow to federal climate authority. It is not the end of the road.

The courts are already moving — and the footing is strong

A broad coalition — NRDC, Center for Biological Diversity, Environmental Defense Fund, Sierra Club, Public Citizen, American Lung Association, Union of Concerned Scientists, and Earthjustice — filed suit in the D.C. Circuit within days. The legal argument is structurally solid: the 2007 Supreme Court ruling in Massachusetts v. EPA established that greenhouse gases are covered by the Clean Air Act. The EPA's repeal attempts to relitigate arguments the Supreme Court already considered and rejected. A federal judge has also already ruled that the administration violated federal law in how it assembled the scientific case for this repeal — handing over more than 100,000 documents that now form the evidentiary record for litigation.

"
Since the United States Environmental Protection Agency issued its Endangerment Finding in 2009, the scientific evidence connecting greenhouse gas emissions to health impacts has only grown stronger. Reversing it wouldn't change the science — it would only make it harder to mitigate the risks we're already facing. Stability matters. Predictability matters. Maintaining science-based policies isn't just prudent — it's the reliable foundation we need to build a safer, healthier and more resilient nation.
Katharine Hayhoe, Chief Scientist, The Nature Conservancy
The Nature Conservancy, Feb 12, 2026
The economics work against the repeal

According to EPA's own prior analysis — documented by NRDC — the 2024 car standards would have cut nearly 8 billion tons of greenhouse gas emissions over three decades and delivered $13 billion a year in avoided healthcare costs between 2026 and 2055. The EPA's own repeal analysis found eliminating vehicle standards will increase gas prices and force Americans to spend more on fuel. The $1.3 trillion savings claim accounts for none of this — and the business community making 10-year capital decisions knows it.

"
Clean car standards are a win-win proposition. They address the largest source of climate pollution, while also saving drivers on their fuel and maintenance costs. Repealing them might appease the oil industry, but the rest of us will suffer with more air pollution, more extreme storms and heat waves — and more trips to fill up at the gas station.
Kathy Harris, Clean Vehicles Director, NRDC
NRDC Press Release, Feb 12, 2026
Three things to watch right now

The D.C. Circuit litigation, which carries a strong evidentiary record built over sixteen years. California's separate vehicle standards lawsuit, which covers 17 states representing roughly 40% of the US auto market — untouched by the federal repeal. And the EU's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism, now more directly relevant than ever to US exporters, which creates direct financial incentives to track emissions regardless of what the EPA mandates domestically.

The fight relocated. It did not end.

We Knew This Was Coming. That Doesn't Make It Less of a Disaster.

The EPA repealed the Endangerment Finding on February 12. If you've been in this field for more than five years, you saw this coming the moment the administration took office. Knowing it was coming doesn't make it land any softer. It just means we don't get to be surprised on top of everything else.

Let's be precise about what was actually taken away, because most coverage has been too gentle about the scale of it.

"
After a year of deadly floods, devastating wildfires, excruciating heat waves, and a growing number of unnatural disasters, the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency made the incomprehensible determination that it does not have the legal authority to address climate change.
NRDC, Official Statement, February 12, 2026
NRDC Press Release, Feb 12, 2026

This is not a rollback of a single regulation. This is the removal of the scientific and legal foundation on which regulations were built. The emissions rules for cars, power plants, and oil and gas facilities don't disappear automatically — but they now have no solid legal ground to stand on. Each one becomes individually vulnerable. Death by a thousand legal challenges, each one easier to mount than it was two weeks ago.

The cost-savings claim deserves to be named precisely

The EPA claimed the repeal saves Americans $1.3 trillion. Its own analysis found that eliminating vehicle standards will increase gas prices and force Americans to spend more on fuel. The 2024 car rules alone would have delivered $13 billion a year in avoided healthcare costs between 2026 and 2055, per EPA's own previous modelling. In 2025, the United States suffered 23 extreme weather events with damages exceeding $1 billion each. None of those costs appear in the $1.3 trillion figure. That is a deliberate choice about which numbers get counted — and who absorbs the ones that don't.

"
Today, Administrator Zeldin took a chainsaw to the Endangerment Finding, undoing this long-standing, science-based finding on bogus grounds at the expense of our health. Instead of rising to the challenge with necessary policies to protect people's wellbeing, the Trump administration has shamefully abandoned EPA's mission and caved to the whims of deep-pocketed special interests. Sacrificing people's health, safety and futures for polluters' profits is unconscionable.
Dr. Gretchen Goldman, President & CEO, Union of Concerned Scientists
UCS Press Release, Feb 12, 2026
"
The repeal of the EPA's endangerment finding is illegal, and if allowed to stand, it will have devastating impacts on public health and a livable climate for decades.
Adina Rosenbaum, Attorney, Public Citizen Litigation Group
NRDC / Earthjustice Filing, Feb 18, 2026

There is also this, which matters: a federal judge already declared the Trump administration violated federal law when it secretly formed a "Climate Working Group" tasked with writing the slanted scientific report this entire repeal is built on. UCS and EDF brought that suit and won. The administration was compelled to surrender more than 100,000 documents. The evidentiary record of how this was done is being assembled in public, for use in court.

The people in this field who built the technical case for these regulations — who submitted comments, testified, produced the peer-reviewed science — that work is not erased. It is the record every legal challenge will draw from. It matters that we did it. It matters that we keep doing it.

You can repeal an Endangerment Finding. You cannot repeal the endangerment.
